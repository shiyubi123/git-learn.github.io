Simple tooltips made of pure CSS

Balloon.css lets you add tooltips to elements without JavaScript and in just a few lines of CSS.

Positioning

For positioning, use data-balloon-pos attribute with one of the values: up , down, left or right.

date-ballon="Whats up!" data-balloon-pos="up"
button data-balloon="Whats up!" data-balloon-pos="left"
button data-balloon="Whats up!" data-balloon-pos="right"
button data-balloon="Whats up!" data-balloon-pos="down"

Lenth

By default, tooltips will always remain single-line no matter their length. You can change this behavior by using the attributedata-balloon-length with one of the values: small , medium, large or fit.

data-balloon-length="small" data-balloon="Hi." data-balloon-pos="up"
data-balloon-length="medium" data-balloon="Hi."data-balloon-pos="up"
data-balloon-length="large" data-balloon="Hi."data-balloon-pos="up"
data-balloon-length="Xlarge" data-balloon="Hi."data-balloon-pos="up"
data-balloon-length="fit" data-balloon="Hi."data-balloon-pos="up"

Lenth

If you want to show tooltips even when user interaction isn't happening, you can simply use the balloon-visibleattribute:

data-balloon-visible data-balloon="I am always visible!" data-balloon-pos="up"

The attributedata-balloon-visible can be easily added via JavaScript (e.g..setAttribute()), enabling you to show tooltips whenever needed.

Drawbacks

Balloon.css make use of pseudo-elements thus self-closing elements such as <img>, <input> and <hr> will not render tooltips.

Also keep in mind that if pseudo elements are already in use on an element, the tooltip will conflict with them resulting in potential bugs.
